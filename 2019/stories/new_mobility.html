New Mobility in Seattle

Findings from the 2019 Household Travel Survey

What is new mobility?

“New mobility are those emerging elements of our transportation system that are enabled by digital technology, shared, driven by real-time data, and often providing curb-to-curb transportation. It allows Seattleites to treat urban transportation as a customizable, on-demand service. They can book and pay for different transportation services as they go, based on what they need.” - Seattle Department of Transportation’s New Mobility Program

The household travel survey program and new mobility

The City of Seattle and Puget Sound Regional Council conduct household travel surveys to understand how residents are currently using the transportation system. The travel survey collect day-to-day information from households in the central Puget Sound region about how and where we travel. The travel survey asked several questions of households about their new mobility use, including:

  • How often people used ridehailing apps and carsharing
  • Which trips they used ridehailing apps and carsharing for
  • How often their household received packages and deliveries

Who is using ridehailing apps, carsharing services, and home delivery services? Who is not? Is there any systematic bias in who uses these services?
